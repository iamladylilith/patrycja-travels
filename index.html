Patricia's Travels

for friends, family & the chronically nosy  ✈

◆

Welcome. If you found this page, I like you enough to let you see all of it — the stunning views, the overexposed photos, the shaky camera work, and at least one incident per trip I'll probably never fully explain.

This is where I archive my adventures so you can live vicariously while I pretend to be a travel influencer — no ring light, no itinerary guides, just me and a carry-on I somehow always manage to overpack.

No brand deals. No sponsored content. (Yet. 👉🏻👈🏻) Just really good trips and a genuine inability to leave anything behind. Enjoy.

My Adventures

Proof that I actually went to all these places

▶
2026  ·  Spain

Mallorca with Kasia

One girls' week. Perfect beaches, perfect company, and one tree that had the audacity to win. I have stitches. The tree does not. 10/10 for everything except the tree.

▶
2026  ·  Maldives

Maldives

Two weeks of turquoise water, non-stop activities, and a genuinely alarming amount of tuna. Arrived as a stranger. Left with a WhatsApp group I actually check.

▶
2023 & 2026  ·  France

Paris

Two trips, zero times I wasn't completely charmed. Paris should be a cliché by now but somehow refuses to be one. The baguettes are fresh, the attitude is real, and every arrondissement feels like its own tiny country. Je reviendrai.

▶
2022–2026  ·  Belgium

Brussels

Three visits and I am still working through the waffle situation. Brussels lets Amsterdam and Paris take the spotlight while quietly being one of the most interesting cities in Europe. The frites are non-negotiable. The beer list is dangerous.

▶
2025  ·  Austria

Vienna

Coffee houses, imperial palaces, and Sachertorte that genuinely changed my relationship with chocolate cake. A city that has been beautiful since before it was fashionable to care about beauty, and it knows it.

▶
2025  ·  Thailand

Thailand with Lolek

Three weeks, one best friend, and approximately one million scooter kilometres. The Full Moon Party in Koh Phangan was everything they said it would be and nothing I was prepared for.

▶
2023 & 2025  ·  Italy

Milan

The Duomo, the aperitivo hour, and Lake Como just an hour away — where mountains drop straight into the water and you forget the city entirely. A place that makes you walk faster, dress better, and escape to the lake on weekends without apologising.

▶
2019 & 2025  ·  USA

New England

I didn't just visit — I lived here for a few months as an au pair, which means I got the real version. October foliage that genuinely doesn't look real, covered bridges, apple orchards, and the kind of autumn that turns into a whole personality.

▶
2018–2025  ·  USA

New York

The city that gives you everything and also all of your anxiety. Delis at 3am, subway platform heat, and someone in Central Park playing an instrument you've never seen. Multiple visits and it somehow gets bigger every time.

▶
2024  ·  Italy

Amalfi Coast

The most beautiful road I have ever been terrified on. Cliffs and lemons and pastel houses stacked because someone ran out of flat land. The limoncello is obligatory. The vertigo is free.

▶
2023  ·  USA

California Road Trip

Two weeks, gorgeous sunsets, LA nights, and one car tow that absolutely nobody saw coming. 10/10 would road trip again, maybe with a better parking strategy.

▶
2023  ·  Italy

Rome

Pasta, history, more pasta, and gelato three times a day because you're on holiday and nobody can stop you. Every corner has a fountain and every fountain has at least one tourist in it.

▶
2023  ·  Denmark

Copenhagen

Impossibly cool, architecturally perfect, and expensive enough to make you cry into your craft beer. The pastries are so good you'll reorganise your entire personality around them.

▶
2023  ·  Italy

Sicily

A volcanic island that smells of orange blossoms and makes the best arancini on earth. Etna, ancient temples, and enough seafood to feed a small navy. Sicily doesn't ask for your attention — it demands it.

▶
2023  ·  Scotland

Edinburgh

A city built for autumn. Cobblestones and castle silhouettes in the mist, closes and wynds that feel straight out of Harry Potter, and the whole city leaning into Halloween like nowhere else I've been. I came for the spooky season and left completely enchanted.

▶
2020  ·  Greece

Rhodes

Sun, sea, ancient ruins, and a very suspicious tan line. Turquoise water, excellent wine, and the kind of heat that turns you into a very willing nap enthusiast.
